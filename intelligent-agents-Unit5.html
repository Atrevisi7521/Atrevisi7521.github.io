Collaborative Discussion 2: Agent Communication Languages

This discussion will last for 3 weeks, starting in Unit 5 and ending in Unit 7. Ensure you include appropriate citations and references in all your posts.

Discussion Topic

What are the potential advantages and disadvantages of the use of agent communication languages such as KQML? How do they compare with method invocation in Python or Java?

Initial Post

Dear Colleagues,

KQML, or Knowledge Query Manipulation Language acts as a communication framework that gives agents a standard way to package their messages and clearly indicate their purpose or intent when communicating with other agents. (Wooldridge, 2009: 136).

There are few advantages and, of course, disadvantages. We could discuss, when talking about advantages, about:

- Interoperability: KQML is able to make different agents (written in different languages and running on different platforms) to communicate with each other effectively.

- loose coupling: almost full independence among components of the same designed architecture and communication through standardized interface and not through direct communication.

- Complexity: as explained wisely by Jagga and Singh (2016) “a communication language should be declarative and simple so that it can be easily understood by its users.” In the case of KQML this requires some improvement because requires more complicated message handling compared to more straightforward function calls.

Other method invocations (in Phyton and Java) are faster more direct but limited to a single application. So, we can say at the end of this discussion that KQML, despite the fact of being slower and maybe not straightforward, is more focused on flexibility of the autonomous systems.

Andrea.
