Welcome to Week 7.

Artificial Neural Network (ANN) is a biology inspired algorithm that, like animal brain, can learn from the environment and adapt itself to make more correct decisions.

At present, ANN and its variant algorithm is used widely in different areas of our lives, from marketing to automation. ANN is a key driver for the industry 4.0 revolution, where machines are making decisions with minimal or no human interactions.

This week we shall learn about:

  • The analogy between biological neuron and artificial neuron network.
  • A detailed understanding of the algorithm underpinning artificial neural networks (ANN).
  • Different functions used in ANN.

On completion of this week, you will be able to:

  • Gain a critical and detailed understanding of the ANN.
  • Design and develop simple ANN artefacts.
  • Critique and contextualise emerging research in ANN.

Personal Reflection

Artificial Neural Networks represent a transformative leap in machine learning, mimicking the human brain's interconnected neuron structure to solve complex problems that traditional algorithms struggle with.

ANNs excel at recognizing patterns, processing non-linear relationships, and adapting through experience, making them invaluable for tasks ranging from image recognition to natural language processing.

The significance of ANNs extends far beyond academic research, driving the Industry 4.0 revolution by enabling autonomous decision-making systems in manufacturing, healthcare, finance, and transportation.

Their ability to learn from vast datasets and improve performance iteratively makes them essential for handling the complexity of modern data challenges.

"Understanding ANNs provides the foundational knowledge necessary for advanced deep learning techniques, including convolutional neural networks and recurrent neural networks.

As businesses increasingly rely on intelligent automation and predictive analytics, proficiency in neural network concepts becomes crucial for developing AI solutions that can adapt, learn, and operate with minimal human intervention in our rapidly evolving technological landscape."
