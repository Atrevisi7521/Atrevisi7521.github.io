Unit 2: Introducing First Order Logic

📅 Week 2
🎓 PgDip AI · Essex
✓ Completed
∀ Introduction

During this week we have the first seminar for the module and focus on discussing the fundamental elements of first order — or predicate — logic. Students engage with this underpinning mathematical notation to gain a greater understanding of some of the key elements of intelligent agents and wider topics.

🎯 This Week We Shall Learn About
  • The key terms and symbols used in first order logic
  • The relationship between first order logic and natural language
  • The use of quantifiers within first order logic
On Completion of This Week, You Will Be Able To
  • Understand the core elements of first order logic
  • Develop an awareness of creating and reasoning over first order logic

Reply to Jaafar El Komati

Dear Jaafar, thank you for taking the time to go through my post and reply. I found your reply very well-articulated and insightful — I really enjoyed reading it.

I agree that better scalability, adaptability, and improved fault tolerance are solid benefits of agent-based systems. Fault tolerance is a key topic — to successfully use multi-agent systems in IoT applications, the systems must be able to handle massive numbers of devices (thousands to millions) while continuing to work even when some devices fail (Dähling et al., 2021).

Another important topic is understanding what organisational hurdles companies might face when implementing these systems. Building organisational readiness is crucial for successful AI implementation, requiring both the right technology and trained employees (Marocco et al., 2024).

Also, we should consider which sectors benefit most from agent-based systems. Based on my experience, agent-based simulation is becoming more widely used in transportation and logistics, and there are now specialised platforms designed specifically for these problems (Gómez-Cruz et al., 2017).

In conclusion, there are still many opportunities for companies to improve with agent-based systems — yet many challenges to overcome for a fruitful implementation.

— Andrea

Reply to Abdulrahman Alhashmi

Dear Abdulrahman, thank you for contributing to this discussion with a remarkably interesting and insightful reply.

I agree about the need to implement proper safeguard systems when deploying agent-based systems in automation. Without those safeguards, agent-based systems could become a liability instead of an asset. As AI agents become more advanced, they face new security risks — requiring strong protection measures to keep these systems safe from threats that could damage their performance or cause harm (Leng et al., 2022).

Your suggestion of human-in-the-loop validation is pertinent to the concept of Industry 5.0. While Industry 4.0 prioritises automated manufacturing processes, Industry 5.0 involves harmonious collaboration between humans and self-operating machines (Nahavandi, 2019). This allows a softer shift towards AI while maintaining human control — giving stakeholders more confidence towards this transition.

However, while human-in-the-loop is a valid solution, a real challenge lies with management itself — managers create the final barrier through their reluctance to embrace new methods and their lack of essential agent-based system competencies (Halaška & Šperka, 2018).

I would like to discuss other evident obstacles to correct agent-based system implementation.

— Andrea
