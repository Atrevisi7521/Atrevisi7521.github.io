Unit 10: Natural Language Processing

Welcome to Week 10.

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is one of the most rapidly evolving fields within machine learning, enabling AI-driven text processing, language understanding, and conversation systems.

The evolution of Transformer-based architectures such as BERT, GPT, and T5 has significantly improved machine understanding of human language. NLP advancements have led to breakthroughs in chatbots, machine translation, information retrieval, and summarisation.

This week, we will explore the advancements in NLP and how machine learning techniques are shaping its future. We will also link these state-of-the-art NLP architectures to evaluation techniques, ensuring a deeper understanding of how NLP models are optimised and assessed.

This week we shall learn about:

  • The evolution of NLP models (from rule-based systems to deep learning).
  • TTransformer-based architectures (BERT, GPT, T5) and their applications.
  • The impact of self-supervised learning on NLP advancements.
  • Evaluation metrics for NLP models, including BLEU, ROUGE, and perplexity.
  • How MLOps is applied to NLP, including model monitoring and retraining.

On completion of this week, you will be able to:

  • Understand the core advancements in NLP and how they are transforming industries.
  • Apply modern NLP models using Transformer architectures.
  • Evaluate NLP models using domain-specific metrics.
  • Critically assess and improve NLP models using hyperparameter tuning and transfer learning.

This discussion will last for 3 weeks covering units 8, 9 and 10. Ensure you include appropriate citations and references in all your posts (this is not included in the word count for each week's post/submission).

Discussion Topic

Read/listen to the article by Hutson (2021) in Nature on Robo-writers. This week, post your thoughts on the risks and benefits of the use AI writers at different levels, from administrative work to creative writing.

You should demonstrate that you understand the topic covered and ensure you use references to academic literature (journals, books, reports, etc.).

Summary post

Dear Colleagues,

Throughout this discussion, my participation has centred on critically examining the dual nature of AI writing tools like GPT-3, Claude, and WriteCream across different application domains. My analysis has evolved from broad risk-benefit considerations to a more context-specific evaluations.

My initial post established the foundational tension between AI writing tools' significant advantages—automated writing routines, accurate drafts, and creative inspiration—and their critical limitations, particularly the lack of contextual understanding leading to potentially dangerous outputs. I emphasized the urgent need for ethical regulatory frameworks, questioning whether these should be universal or company specific.

Through engaging with colleagues Dalbir Singh and Choyee Wolff, I expanded this analysis to address professional creative industries and educational contexts. I highlighted concerns raised by professional writers regarding training data consent and deepfakes, demonstrating how AI threatens creative enterprise broadly.

This engagement revealed my evolving position that AI tools are most beneficial for administrative and mechanical tasks while requiring significant human oversight for creative applications. Nevertheless, I can say that, for writing purposes, it can be a help for someone who is suffering of writer’s block syndrome. As highlighted by Hart-Davis (2023) “At such times, you can reach for ChatGPT and break through the block in mere moments”.

My final contribution emphasized the critical role of institutional guidance, particularly in educational settings. I argued that students cannot navigate AI ethics independently, making instructor leadership essential for establishing appropriate usage guidelines.

During this discussion, the discussion forum panel demonstrate understanding that machine learning techniques in AI writing tools present context-dependent risks and benefits.

While these tools excel at pattern recognition and text generation, their limitations in contextual understanding and ethical reasoning necessitate human supervision, particularly in creative and academic applications. The technical uncertainty inherent in these systems requires robust institutional frameworks rather than individual user discretion.

Personal Reflection

Natural Language Processing represents the pinnacle of AI's quest to bridge human-computer communication, transforming how machines understand, interpret, and generate human language.

The revolutionary impact of Transformer architectures like BERT, GPT, and T5 has fundamentally changed NLP capabilities, enabling sophisticated applications from intelligent chatbots and real-time translation to automated content generation and semantic search systems.

Understanding modern NLP techniques is essential for developing AI solutions that can process unstructured text data, extract meaningful insights, and interact naturally with users.

These capabilities are increasingly vital across industries, powering customer service automation, content analysis, sentiment monitoring, and knowledge extraction from vast document repositories.

Mastering NLP and its evaluation methodologies provides the foundation for creating robust language models that can handle diverse linguistic challenges, cultural nuances, and domain-specific terminology.

"As organizations increasingly rely on text-based data analysis and conversational AI interfaces, proficiency in NLP becomes crucial for developing intelligent systems that can understand context, maintain coherent dialogues, and deliver accurate language-based solutions in our increasingly connected digital world".
