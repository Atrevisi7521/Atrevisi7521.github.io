Dear Lauretta,

Thank you for taking the time to read my initial post and thanks for the insightful reply which I really enjoy reading. You raise valid points about the practical deployment challenges of OWL 2 in large-scale agent systems.

OWL 2's effectiveness depends on its implementation approach, not just theory. Highlighting OWL 2 profiles is important since they let developers balance expressiveness and computational efficiency for web-scale applications.

Separating ontological reasoning from querying aligns with modern semantic architecture. Using OWL 2 for reasoning, SPARQL for queries, and SWRL or RIF for rules allows agents to benefit from advanced reasoning while specialised query engines ensure performance.

However, I would argue that this modular flexibility is precisely what makes OWL 2 valuable: its ability to integrate within broader semantic stacks whilst providing standardised ontological foundations. The profile-driven approach you mention does not diminish OWL 2's usefulness but rather demonstrates its adaptability to varying application requirements (Kr√∂tzsch, 2012).
