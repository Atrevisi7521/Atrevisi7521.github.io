🔍 Introduction

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is valuable to data science and AI projects since it allows us to get closer to the certainty that future results will be valid, correctly interpreted, and applicable to the desired business contexts. Such certainty can be achieved only after raw data is validated and checked for anomalies, ensuring the dataset was collected without errors.

EDA also helps to find insights that were not evident or worth investigating to business stakeholders and data scientists but can be very informative about a particular business.

🎯 This Week We Shall Learn About
  • The steps involved with EDA
  • Understanding the dataset through feature exploration
  • How to spot anomalies within the dataset
  • Visual analysis of the dataset

Hi Choyee, thanks for your insightful post and for sharing this incident — a particularly good example of how unexpected downtime can cause significant issues to a company's operations, resulting in lost customer trust.

I would like to share an incident which I think is important to mention when talking about AI failing — not properly a technical failure but mostly an ethical and legal failure of an AI chatbot. After an NBA game, the poor performance of Klay Thompson was mocked by fans on "X". Many posts joked about Thompson "throwing bricks at windows," a playful reference to his shooting. The AI chatbot "Grok" took this literally and accused the player of acts of vandalism, stating he was throwing bricks at people's windows in California. The platform had to release an immediate apology.

Even if resolved quickly, it raised many questions about responsible AI development with robust verification mechanisms, especially in Industry 4.0 environments where automated systems make consequential decisions. As reported by Rese and Witthohn (2025: 12) "the potential of chatbots to increase a company's efficiency is hampered by their susceptibility to errors, which often leads to user expectations of chatbots not being met."

Do you think, based on this example, that DevSecOps integration is essential — ensuring that security, ethical considerations, and quality assurance are built into AI systems from the ground up rather than treated as afterthoughts?

// Personal Reflection

Exploratory Data Analysis has been quite a challenging topic to handle — but I learned it is a fundamental tool. EDA is a crucial preliminary step in any AI/machine learning pipeline that involves:

Understanding the structure and characteristics of your data before building AI models. Identifying patterns, trends, and relationships in the dataset. Detecting outliers, missing values, and data quality issues. Determining which features might be most relevant for your AI model.

I am really looking forward to start working practically on some datasets and trying Exploratory Data Analysis myself.

"EDA is like getting to know your data intimately before you ask it to power an AI system — it's the foundation that makes everything else more reliable and effective."
