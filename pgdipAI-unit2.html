Welcome to Week 2.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is valuable to data science (and AI) projects since it allows to get closer to the certainty that the future results will be valid, correctly interpreted, and applicable to the desired business contexts. Such level of certainty can be achieved only after raw data is validated and checked for anomalies, ensuring that the data set was collected without errors. EDA also helps to find insights that were not evident or worth investigating to business stakeholders and data scientists but can be very informative about a particular business.

This week we shall learn about:

  • The steps involved with EDA.
  • Understanding the dataset through feature exploration.
  • How to spot anomalies within the dataset.
  • Visual analysis of the dataset.

On completion of this week, you will be able to:

  • Undertake basic EDA.
  • Understand the dataset.
  • Prepare the dataset for machine learning.

Personal Reflection

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) has been quite a challenging topic to handle. But I learned that is a fundamental tool to learn. EDA is a crucial preliminary step in any AI/machine learning pipeline that involves:

Understanding the structure and characteristics of your data before building AI models

Identifying patterns, trends, and relationships in the dataset

Detecting outliers, missing values, and data quality issues

Determining which features might be most relevant for your AI model

I am really looking forward to start working practically on some datasets and try the Exploratory Data Analysis myself.

"EDA is like getting to know your data intimately before you ask it to power an AI system - it's the foundation that makes everything else more reliable and effective."
