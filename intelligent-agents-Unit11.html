// Collaborative Discussion 3 · Units 9–11 · Summary Post
Deep Learning — Discussion Summary

Dear Colleagues, this discussion forum has been enlightening and insightful, providing many valuable starting points to share.

In my initial post I identified four important ethical concerns around deep learning technologies such as DALL-E and ChatGPT: Copyright and Intellectual Property, Bias and Discrimination, Misinformation, and Labour Displacement (Trevisi, 2025).

The topic of misinformation is becoming increasingly pressing with the proliferation of deepfakes and AI-generated media content, which are barely discernible from real footage. This is a serious threat — especially in a tense sociopolitical environment where most people receive news through social media. As Carpenter (2025) notes, when it comes to deepfakes and AI image generation, the unintended consequences can be a world filled with convincing lies serving the darkest of goals.

With Jaco Espag, we explored the difficult topic of exploited workers — such as those on Amazon Mechanical Turk — who endure long hours for little compensation to maintain AI infrastructures. With Fabian Narel, we discussed copyright, intellectual property, and the challenge of protecting creative work from deepfake misuse.

More broadly, this discussion raised important questions about AI governance. Many colleagues believe that official regulatory frameworks — such as the EU AI Act — represent a solid starting point to limit misuse of deep learning technologies. But could that be enough?

— Andrea
