Welcome to Week 11.

This unit will bring together several of the core subjects that have been covered to date, such as agent-based computing, adaptive algorithms and deep learning. This week focuses on how these technologies are currently being used in practice in manufacturing and Industry 4.0, specifically manufacturing and FinTech, to great effect.

This week we shall learn about:

  • Introduce the concept of Industry 4.0 and smart manufacturing.
  • Explore the concept of a smart shop floor and the associated issues.
  • Investigate the use of agent-based modelling in the financial sector.

On completion of this week, you will be able to:

  • Apply the concepts and theories learned to date to specific sectors.
  • Understand how technology can be used to drive efficiency.
  • Evaluate the relative merits of these new approaches in context.

Summary Post

Dear Colleagues,

This last discussion forum has been enlightening and insightful and gave me a lot of starting points to share with you.

In my first post in the forum, I pointed out that there are few important ethical concerns around the usage of Deep learning technologies such as DALL-E or ChatGPT. I took into consideration the following issues:

- Copyright and Intellectual Property

- Bias and Discrimination

- Misinformation

- Labor Displacement

(Trevisi, 2025).

Especially the topic of the misinformation is becoming actual with the increasing of deepfake and AI generated media content, which are, barely discernible from real videos. This is a big threat, especially in a tense sociopolitical situation where most of the people get news from social media. As reported by Carpenter (2025) ‚Äúwhen it comes to deepfakes and whim-based image generation, the unintended consequences can be a world filled with convincing lies serving the darkest of goals and imaginations.‚Äù

We continue an interesting discussion with Jaco Espag where we touch the tough topic of the exploited workers (i.e. the Amazon Mechanical Turk) that suffers long hours of work and little compensation to check AI infrastructures.

Another interesting topic was the one suggested by Fabian Narel in his starting contribution to the forum. He also thinks that an important topic to take into consideration is the one related to copyrights and intellectual property and how to protect that intellectual property once misused with deepfakes.

Generally speaking, this discussion forum has raised questions and concerns about the ethical issues that the deep learning technologies are presenting. Many of my fellow-students thinks that official government acts (like the European Union AI Act) could be a very solid start to try to limit the misuse of the deep learning technologies but, could it be enough ?

Thanks,

Andrea.
