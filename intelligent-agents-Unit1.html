Unit 1: Introduction to Agent-Based Computing

Completed

Welcome to Week 1.

We will be setting the agenda by looking at the trends in computing that have led to the rise of agent-based computing. We will also introduce the concept of an agent, what it is and why they have gained popularity across a breadth of sectors and applications.

This week we shall learn about:

  • Look at the trends in computing that have led to the growth in agent-based technologies.
  • Investigate the implications of these growing trends and their impact on the wider landscape.
  • Explore agents and how they can interact with the environment and each other.
  • Define examples of agent-based systems and their applications.

On completion of this week, you will be able to:

  • Develop the understanding of key concepts of agent-based computing.
  • Understand the trends that have facilitated the development of agents as a paradigm.
  • Compare different types of agent-based system to contrast their relative merits.

Collaborative Discussion 1: Agent Based Systems

This discussion will last for 3 weeks, starting in Unit 1 and ending in Unit 3. Ensure you include appropriate citations and references in all your posts.

Discussion Topic

Discuss what has led to the rise of agent-based systems and the benefits that this approach can offer to organisations. Discussion should include at least three references.

Initial Post

Dear Colleagues,

Main reasons why the agent-based systems are rising are to be found in the increased needs, from organisations, to increase their degree of automations, when talking about AI implementation and to decrease the human intervention.

As reported by Hughes et al. (2025) “as organizations pursue higher levels of automation, the adoption and integration of AI agents and agentic systems hold considerable potential for business transformation.”

The advantages of automation are visible in terms of time saving, process automation, reduced processing time and complex problem solving. “The aim is to maximise the quality and performance of the outcome while minimising the overall costs of the process.” (Herrera et al., 2020).

Another important point to be mentioned when discussing the rising of agent-based systems in organisations is the cost saving, in fact, the most important features of multi-agent systems are, among other things, cost efficiency, low cost in terms of maintenance, reliability and a significant save of money and effective solutions when it comes to solve complicated tasks (Dorri et al., 2018).

Here we have discussed the benefits of the introduction of the agent-based systems in the current organisation, and I would like to know from all of you if we can mention few other benefits.

Thanks,

Andrea.
