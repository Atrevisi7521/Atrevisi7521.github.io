Postgraduate Diploma in Artificial Intelligence - Intelligent Agents (July 2025)

I am currently pursuing a Postgraduate Diploma in Artificial Intelligence at the University of Essex (UK). This program is enhancing my business expertise with cutting-edge AI knowledge, allowing me to bridge the gap between business strategy and technological innovation.

This module is called "Intelligent Agents" and consists of 12 comprehensive units covering various aspects of Intelligent Agents, and their practical applications. Below you can explore my journey through each unit, with detailed notes and reflections on what I've learned.

Intelligent Agents Module Structure

The Intelligent Agents Module (April 2025) is structured around 12 weekly units, each focusing on a specific aspect of Intelligent Agents and its applications. The course combines theoretical foundations with practical implementations, preparing students to apply AI solutions in real-world contexts.

Unit 1: Introduction to Agent-Based Computing
We will be setting the agenda by looking at the trends in computing that have led to the rise of agent-based computing. We will also introduce the concept of an agent, what it is and why they have gained popularity across a breadth of sectors and applications.

Unit 2: Introducing First Order Logic
During this week we have the first seminar for the module and will focus on discussing the fundamental elements of first order or predicate logic. It will involve students engaging with this underpinning mathematical notation in order to provide a greater understanding of some of the key elements of intelligent agents and wider topics.
View Notes
Status: Completed
Unit 3: Agent Architectures
This week’s lecturecast focuses on the different approaches available when deciding on an agent architecture. There is a discussion of the history of agents and their development, and how this has led to a number of key architectures. We will explore symbolic reasoning agents, agents with state, reactive architectures and more to understand the value of relative merits of the different approaches.
View Notes
Status: Completed
Unit 4: Hybrid Agent Architectures
In this seminar this week we will be looking at hybrid agent architectures that combine reactive and deliberative behaviours. It will involve looking at the underpinning ideas that form this type of architecture and evaluating its relative merits. This will be underlined with a case study that details the deployment of a hybrid architecture and the reasons for its selection.
View Notes
Status: Completed
Unit 5: Agent Communication
This week’s lecturecast focuses on the way in which agents communicate with each other. The lecturecast also looks at the theory of language with Speech Acts and then provides an example of an agent communication language (KQML). Finally, an introduction to ontologies and the way they can be used in agent communications will be discussed.

Unit 6: Working Together
This week’s seminar focuses on the use agent communication languages, specifically KQML and KIF, as discussed in the previous lecturecast. This will provide you with the chance to put some of the concepts into practice by developing simple dialogues using the common KQML performatives.

Unit 7: Natural Language Processing (NLP)
This week’s lecturecast focuses on one of the most rapidly growing technologies in intelligent agents, which is Natural Language Processing (or NLP). The lecturecast covers some of the background to NLP and why it is hard for machines to understand the way that we talk before diving into some examples of methods and technologies that can be deployed.

This is followed by a formative activity to reinforce the way various parse tree structures are created.
