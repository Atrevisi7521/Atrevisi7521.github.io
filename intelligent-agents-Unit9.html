Initial Post

Dear Colleagues,

Deep learning technologies like DALL-E and ChatGPT raise significant ethical concerns that demand careful consideration.

Copyright and Intellectual Property: AI models are trained on billions of copyrighted works scraped from the internet without permission or compensation. Multiple lawsuits challenge whether this constitutes fair use or infringement. The May report suggests such unauthorized use may constitute prima facie copyright violation (U.S. Copyright Office, 2025).

Bias and Discrimination: These systems inherit biases from training data, potentially perpetuating discrimination in hiring, lending, and resource allocation. In their 2021 Recommendation, The UNESCO emphasizes that without proper guardrails, AI risks reproducing real-world discrimination and threatening human rights (UNESCO, 2021).

Misinformation: Deepfakes and AI-generated content make it increasingly difficult to distinguish authentic from fabricated material, threatening journalism, elections, and public trust.

Labor Displacement: While democratizing creativity, these technologies simultaneously threaten creative professionals' livelihoods, raising questions about the value of human artistry.

In conclusion, these technologies require robust regulations, transparency, and ongoing ethical dialogue to balance innovation with responsibility.I would like to involve all the students and ask them which ethical concern is more worrying at the current status.
