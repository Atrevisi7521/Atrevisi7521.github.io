Collaborative Discussion 1: Agent Based Systems

This discussion will last for 3 weeks, starting in Unit 1 and ending in Unit 3. Ensure you include appropriate citations and references in all your posts.

Discussion Topic

Discuss what has led to the rise of agent-based systems and the benefits that this approach can offer to organisations. Discussion should include at least three references.

Summary Post:

Dear colleagues,

During the last three weeks we have been discussing about the agent-based systems, their introduction in companies and organisations and the benefits and challenges companies are facing during that implementation. Many interesting and insightful posts and replies have made this discussion forum interesting and stimulating.

As defined by Botti et al. (2019: 10) ‚Äúmulti-agent systems are a particular case of a distributed system, and its particularity lies in the fact that components of this system are autonomous and selfish, seeking to satisfy their own objectives‚Äù.

In my first post I started highlighting the importance of the implementation of agent-based systems in today‚Äôs organisations for many reasons, the most important being: cost-saving, timesaving, enhanced automation and efficiency and complex problem solving. Those are fundamental reasons to implement and use this technology nowadays.

I discussed with my colleague, Jaafar El Komati, about two particularly important topics, fault tolerance and system resilience. We both agreed on the paramount importance of those two topics in the implementation of agent-based systems especially taking into consideration the enormous number of devices that the agent-based systems must handle simultaneously. These two topics are critical when considering the implementation.

Another insightful discussion was the one with another colleague, Abdulrahman Alhashmi, about the importance of the human-in-the-loop validation. This validation is especially important to avoid serious issues, misinterpretation of the data, disruptions and, finally, decision-making errors that could have extremely negative results on the daily operation and reputation of an organisation. We finally agreed that human cooperation with agent-based systems is totally in line with the current definition of industry 5.0 where this collaboration human and machine are not only recommended but also desirable.

In conclusion we can say that the introduction of agent-based systems in companies is indeed inevitable, yet needs some attention in order to avoid issues and disruptions.

Thanks,

Andrea
