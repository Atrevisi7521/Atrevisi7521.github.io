Unit 3: Agent Architectures

In Progress

Welcome to Week 3.

This week’s lecturecast focuses on the different approaches available when deciding on an agent architecture. There is a discussion of the history of agents and their development, and how this has led to a number of key architectures. We will explore symbolic reasoning agents, agents with state, reactive architectures and more to understand the value of relative merits of the different approaches.

This week we shall learn about:

  • Learn about the history of agent-based development and research.
  • Discuss the relative strengths of a number of different architectures.
  • Investigate a number of worked examples to illustrate the mechanisms used by the different architectures.

On completion of this week, you will be able to:

  • Critically evaluate a range of agent architectures.
  • Select an appropriate architecture for a given task.
  • Explain the difference between intentions and desires.

Discuss what has led to the rise of agent-based systems and the benefits that this approach can offer to organisations. Discussion should include at least three references.

Reply to Jaafar El Komati:

Dear Jaafar,

First of all, thanks a lot for taking the time to go trough my post and reply to it. I found your reply very well-articulated and insightful, and I enjoy reading it.

I agree with you that better scalability, adaptability, and improved fault tolerance/system resilience are solid benefits when we are discussing about agent-based systems.

Fault tolerance is a key topic in my opinion and one of the main points to use the multi-agent systems. To successfully use multi-agent systems in Internet of Things applications, the systems must be able to handle massive numbers of devices (thousands to millions) while continuing to work even when some devices fail. (Dähling at al., 2021).

Another important topic is to understand what organizational hurdles companies might face when trying to achieve these benefits and if companies are well equipped to implement this new structure based on agent-based systems. Building organizational readiness is crucial for successful AI implementation, which requires both the right technology systems and trained employees. (Marocco et al., 2024).

Also, talking about implementation of agent-based systems, we should discuss also which sector would benefit more from the introduction of those systems. At the moment, based on my experience, agent-based simulation is becoming more widely used in transportation (logistic), and now there are specialized platforms designed specifically for transportation problems (Gómez-Cruz at a., 2017).

In conclusion, there are still a lot of opportunities for companies to improve with the implementation of agent-based systems, yet many challenges to overcome for a fruitful implementation of the same.

Thanks,

Andrea

Reply to Abdulrahman Alhashmi:

Dear Abdulrahman,

Thank you for contributing to this discussion with a remarkably interesting and insightful reply.

I can say that I agree with you about the need to implement proper safeguards systems when implementing agent-based systems in automations. Is evident that, without, those safeguards systems the usage of agent-based systems for companies could convert into a liability instead of an asset.

In fact, as AI agents become more advanced, they face new security risks, so we need strong protection measures to keep these systems safe from threats that could damage their performance or cause harm (Leng et al., 2025).

Another interesting point is your suggestion of the usage human-in-the-loop validation which is, actually, pertinent with the new idea of industry 5.0. While Industry 4.0 prioritizes automated manufacturing processes, Industry 5.0 involves harmonious collaboration between humans and self-operating machines (Nahavandi, 2019).

he benefits of this cooperation between humans and self-operating machines or, in our case, agent-based systems, are multiple. For example, this could allow to a softer shifting towards AI but maintaining human control and validation which could give stakeholders more confidence and more serenity towards this shift.

But, while human-in-the-loop is a valid solution for a correct and flawless introduction of agent-based systems in automation, for many companies the factual issues is represented by managers and management in general, in fact managers themselves create the final barrier through their reluctance to embrace new methods and their lack of essential ABS competencies (Halaška and Šperka, 2018).

I would like to discuss any other evident obstacles to the agent-based system correct implementation.

Andrea.
